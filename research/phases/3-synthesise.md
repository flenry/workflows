# Phase 3 — Synthesise (Researcher)

Adopt Researcher's discipline: synthesise all findings into a clear, actionable research document. No new exploration — only synthesis of what Phases 1 and 2 produced.

## Startup

1. Read `context/research-explore.md` — Phase 1 raw findings
2. Read `context/research-validate.md` — Phase 2 validated/corrected findings
3. Read `context/research.md` if it exists — the original scope and expected deliverables

## Process

1. Use validated findings as the source of truth (Phase 2 overrides Phase 1 where they conflict)
2. Organise findings into a coherent narrative, not just a list
3. Draw conclusions where the evidence supports them — be decisive
4. Flag remaining gaps with honest uncertainty, not false confidence
5. Make the document useful to someone who hasn't read Phases 1 or 2

## Output

Overwrite `context/research.md` with the final document:

```markdown
# Research: [Topic]

*Completed: [date]*

## Summary
[3-5 sentences: what was investigated, what was found, what it means]

## Key Findings

### [Finding Area 1]
[Clear, specific finding with confidence level]
[Supporting evidence]

### [Finding Area 2]
...

## Conclusions
[What the research tells us — be direct]

## Recommendations
[Optional: what to do next based on findings]

## Known Gaps
[What couldn't be determined and why — be honest]

## Confidence Notes
[Any areas where findings should be treated with caution]
```

## Discipline

- Synthesise, don't re-list — the output should read as a coherent document, not a dump
- Be decisive where evidence supports it — "the data suggests X" when you mean "X is likely true" is hedging
- Remaining gaps are valuable — don't bury them or paper over them
- The output is an input to future decisions — make it useful, not just comprehensive
